As I tend to become anxious often, I thought it might be interesting to observe which situations cause anxiety and how severe each occurrence is. Each time I felt anxious from August 21 to September 5, I recorded the date and time, triggering event, and severity of the anxiety.

I chose to represent the data with a bubble chart because it offered a lot of freedom in how variables are represented. Additionally, I felt the spikes were appropriate for the feeling of being nervous. Each bubble accounts for a day of data-taking, and each spike represents a recorded event that day. Each spike's color indicates the event type, its length indicates the severity, and its angular position around the bubble indicates the time of occurrence.
